Your wellness questions, answered

Everything you need to begin your mindfulness journey with clarity and confidence.

  • Start with just 5 minutes a day. Find a quiet spot, sit comfortably, and focus on your breath. When your mind wanders (it will), gently return your attention without judgment. Consistency matters far more than duration. Most people notice a difference within two weeks of daily practice.
    Beginner 5 min/day
  • The best time is the time you'll actually do it consistently. Morning sessions help set the tone for the day, while evening sessions can aid in processing and winding down. Avoid meditating immediately after meals when you're most likely to feel drowsy. Experiment for two weeks and trust what your body tells you.
    Morning Evening
  • Slow, deep breathing activates the parasympathetic nervous system — the opposite of your fight-or-flight response. The 4-7-8 technique (inhale 4s, hold 7s, exhale 8s) is clinically shown to reduce cortisol within minutes. Your breath is the only autonomic function you can consciously control, making it the fastest path to calm.
    Science-backed Instant
  • Apps are scaffolding — use them to build the habit, then gradually reduce dependency. Guided sessions are excellent for beginners, and the streaks and reminders boost consistency. But the goal is to meditate anywhere, anytime, without a phone. Treat apps as training wheels, not the destination.
    Guided App-free goal
  • Most people feel calmer and more focused after 1-2 weeks of consistent daily practice. Measurable changes in stress reactivity and sleep quality appear at the 4-8 week mark. Brain scans show structural changes in the amygdala (the stress center) after as few as 8 weeks. Think of it like exercise — the compound returns are real and lasting.
    2 weeks Compound growth
