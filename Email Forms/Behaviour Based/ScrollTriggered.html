This page demonstrates two scroll-triggered subscription patterns. The first is an inline form that fades in when it enters the viewport. The second is a floating corner form that appears after you've scrolled 40% down the page.

Why scroll-triggered forms work

Timing is everything in conversion. A form that appears before a reader has had a chance to decide if your content is worth their email address will be ignored or dismissed. Scroll-triggered forms respect the reader's attention — showing up only after they've demonstrated genuine interest by reading.

"Show the ask only after you've proven the value. Scroll-depth is the clearest signal of engagement you have."

Research consistently shows that scroll-depth-triggered CTAs outperform time-based and exit-intent triggers for content-heavy pages like articles, long-form sales pages, and documentation. The reader has already demonstrated commitment through sustained engagement.

Implementation patterns

Modern implementations use the Intersection Observer API to detect when a form placeholder enters the viewport, or use a simple scroll percentage threshold to trigger floating corner widgets. Both approaches are demonstrated on this page.

The corner widget appears after 40% scroll depth — you may have already seen it appear. The inline form below will animate into view when you reach it.

Keep scrolling to see the inline scroll-triggered form animate in below this text. Notice how the form slides and fades into view when it enters your viewport — this is the Intersection Observer at work.
